About
My Journey

Growing up, I built mini wooden picture frames from leftover wood scraps while my parents were building their first house. I went door to door selling them, pitching a story about how each one was resourcefully made. I didn't know it then, but that was my first product launch.

That instinct to build and sell carried into my career. I started in investment banking in Singapore, advising fintech founders on $60M+ in growth capital and strategic deals. Three years of evaluating products taught me something important: a great product isn't enough. You need the right market, a loyal customer base, and the ability to cross-sell into adjacent needs. When the opportunity came to do exactly that at a global scale with BCG X, I jumped.

At BCG X, I was a product manager within the Financial Institutions group, owning Banking Pools, a global banking revenue intelligence platform. I deployed ML-based products across 150+ markets and built growth and monetization strategies with Fortune 500 banking clients. I developed systems and processes built to last, and shipped at a scale I didn't think was possible. But after five years of global consulting, I felt the pull of India's fastest-growing fintech wave: digital payments. So I shipped myself as fast as I could to join IppoPay.

At IppoPay, I got closer to the ground floor than ever. I owned the merchant lifecycle platform, deployed AI-driven onboarding systems, and defined the roadmap for a $12M lending product.

I recently completed my MBA at Cornell University. Today, I'm at Amazon, leading product launch and commercialization for Amazon Now, covering Western U.S. markets and building the platform to make customers even happier in 30 minutes or less.

I turn ambiguity into products and products into revenue.

1
Discover

Data-Led Problem Discovery

I don't start with assumptions — I start with instrumentation. Before forming a hypothesis, I analyze behavioral data, segment by cohort, and identify patterns that surface the real friction. User interviews validate what the data reveals, not the other way around.

Financial & Market Sizing

Every opportunity gets sized with TAM/SAM/SOM rigor. My investment banking background means I model the economics before committing resources — addressable market, revenue potential, margin structure, and competitive density. If the numbers don't support the thesis, we don't build.

Competitive & Ecosystem Mapping

Understanding where value sits in a payments or fintech stack is critical. I map the competitive landscape, identify platform adjacencies, and find gaps where a new product or feature creates defensible advantage — not just incremental improvement.

2
Define & Build

Tradeoff Decisioning & Saying No

The hardest part of product isn't building — it's deciding what not to build. I use ROI-based prioritization frameworks to kill low-value initiatives early, protect engineering bandwidth, and prevent technical debt before it accumulates. Saying no with data is a PM superpower.

Unit Economics-Driven Prioritization

Features compete on margin impact, not feature requests. I prioritize by modeling unit economics, payback periods, and LTV contribution. Every roadmap item has a financial thesis before it earns a spot in the sprint. If the economics are unclear, we run an experiment first.

Decision Systems Architecture

I build frameworks that scale, not one-off solutions. Whether it's a capital allocation workflow, a pricing engine, or an eligibility model, I design decision systems that serve the next 10 use cases — not just today's request. Platform thinking over feature thinking.

3
Launch & Grow

Experimentation Design

Structured hypothesis-driven A/B tests with clear success criteria, control groups, and guardrail metrics to validate product decisions before full rollout. Every experiment has a predefined kill threshold and a scale-up trigger. Ship fast, measure faster.

Activation & Monetization Strategy

Designing onboarding funnels, pricing triggers, and conversion levers that connect product engagement directly to revenue outcomes. The goal is reducing time-to-value for users while maximizing willingness-to-pay at each stage of adoption.

Retention & Lifecycle Optimization

Building measurement frameworks across the user lifecycle — activation, engagement, retention — using cohort analysis, CSAT instrumentation, and behavioral segmentation. Sustainable growth comes from retention loops, not acquisition volume.
